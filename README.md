## Game Intructions

At its core, the experience builds on the familiar mechanics of a classic memory game, with a creative twist.

A key is provided showing the pairs players are trying to locate, with each match representing a word or phrase prompt. Players take turns flipping two cards at a time.
	•	If the cards match, the player keeps the pair and continues their turn.
	•	If the cards do not match, the next player takes their turn.

Once a match is found, players act out the prompt together in real life, turning the game into a shared sensory experience that blends digital play with physical connection.

The game ends when all pairs have been found or when the turn limit is reached. Depending on the level selected, players have between 25 and 35 turns, with 35 being the maximum. The player who collects the most pairs wins.

## Relationship Levels

To keep the experience engaging and adaptable, the game includes different relationship stages:
	•	Dating – playful prompts designed for new connections
	•	Committed – building trust and deepening familiarity
	•	Honeymoon – passionate, exploratory moments
	•	Married – rediscovering spark and connection
	•	Child at Heart – lighthearted, silly, and joy-filled prompts

Each level offers a slightly different tone, allowing players to choose the experience that best fits their relationship.


## Why this Project Exists

This project is an extension of my original memory game, “Pair It to Win It.”
This version introduces a playful, slightly risqué twist designed for adults. Players match prompts in-app and then bring them to life through real-world touch—blending play, curiosity, connection, and embodied experience.

For over two decades working in wellness, I’ve been a strong advocate for the power of human touch. Technology isn’t going anywhere. The real opportunity is learning how to design it in ways that bring people closer together instead of pulling them further apart.

Science continues to reinforce what many of us instinctively know: safe, consensual touch is powerful. Physical touch has been shown to lower cortisol levels, the body’s primary stress hormone, while also increasing oxytocin, the hormone associated with bonding, trust, and emotional connection. These responses help regulate the nervous system, reduce stress, and strengthen relationships.

In other words, touch doesn’t just feel good,it supports real biological and emotional well-being.

This game was created with that philosophy in mind. It uses a familiar memory game format as a gateway to something deeper: encouraging couples to pause, play, laugh, explore, and reconnect with one another in a meaningful way.

The technology starts the interaction.

But the real experience happens off the screen.

Because sometimes the most powerful thing technology can do…
is remind us to reach for each other instead.

## ⚙️ Tech Stack & Architecture

This project is built as a Progressive Web App (PWA) to provide an app-like experience while remaining lightweight and accessible across devices.

Core Features
	•	Progressive Web App (PWA)
Installable directly from the browser and usable across mobile and desktop devices.
	•	Offline Capability
Service workers allow the game to function even when offline once installed.
	•	Local Storage–First Design
Player data, progress, and preferences are stored locally on the user’s device, giving players full control over their information.
	•	Privacy-Focused Architecture
No automatic cloud storage or external data collection.
	•	PDF Export
Users can download their scores or session data as a PDF file if they choose to keep track of their progress.
	•	Relationship-Level Gameplay Modes
Multiple levels designed to match different relationship dynamics.

Game Mechanics
	•	Memory-based card matching system
	•	Turn-based gameplay
	•	Variable turn limits depending on difficulty level
	•	Prompt-triggered real-world interactions

Design Philosophy

This project follows a human-centered design approach, where technology acts as a facilitator rather than the primary experience.

The app creates the structure.

The connection happens between people.
